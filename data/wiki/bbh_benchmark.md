# BIG-Bench Hard (BBH)

**Type:** dataset

A challenging reasoning benchmark including tasks such as temporal sequences, tracking shuffled objects, and word sorting, used to evaluate cross-task generalization of FoVer-PRMs.

---

BIG-Bench Hard (BBH) is a challenging subset of the BIG-Bench benchmark consisting of tasks that were found to be particularly difficult for large language models at the time of its creation, including problems such as temporal sequence reasoning, tracking shuffled objects, logical deduction, and word sorting. BBH serves as a diverse testbed for evaluating general reasoning capabilities beyond mathematics, making it useful for assessing cross-task generalization. In the FoVer paper, BBH is included in the 12-benchmark evaluation suite to assess whether FoVer-PRMs, trained entirely on formal reasoning data, can improve solution selection on informal and diverse reasoning tasks. FoVer-PRMs demonstrate meaningful performance gains on BBH, providing evidence of broad cross-task generalization.
