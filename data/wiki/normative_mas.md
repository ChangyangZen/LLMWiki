# Normative Multi-Agent Systems

**Type:** concept

A research tradition formalizing how norms govern agent behavior through obligations, prohibitions, and permissions, informing the governance semantics of Agent Contracts.

---

Normative Multi-Agent Systems (Normative MAS) is a research tradition within multi-agent systems that formalizes how social norms govern agent behavior through explicit obligations, prohibitions, and permissions. Frameworks in this tradition, such as those based on deontic logic, specify what agents must do, must not do, and are permitted to do within a regulated environment, enabling the design of accountable and predictable multi-agent societies. Agent Contracts draw on the governance semantics of Normative MAS, treating resource constraints and success criteria as obligations that bound agent autonomy in ways analogous to normative constraints. By grounding Agent Contracts in this tradition, the framework inherits a rich theoretical vocabulary for reasoning about compliance, violation, and accountability in autonomous systems.
