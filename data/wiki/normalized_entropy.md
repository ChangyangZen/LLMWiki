# Normalized Entropy (NE)

**Type:** metric

An uncertainty signal computed from the sampled answer distribution at each turn, used by MiCP to decide whether to stop or continue reasoning.

---

Normalized entropy (NE) is an uncertainty signal computed from the distribution of sampled answers generated by a language model at a given reasoning turn, used in MiCP to assess whether the model is sufficiently confident to stop reasoning. It is derived by clustering semantically equivalent sampled answers and computing the entropy of the resulting cluster frequency distribution, normalized by the log of the number of samples to bound the value between zero and one. Low normalized entropy indicates that sampled answers are concentrated around a single response, suggesting high confidence, while high values indicate uncertainty and motivate continued reasoning. MiCP uses normalized entropy as its nonconformity score to calibrate per-turn stopping thresholds via conformal prediction.
