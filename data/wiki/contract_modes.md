# Contract Modes

**Type:** concept

Predefined contract configurations (URGENT, ECONOMICAL, BALANCED) that operationalize satisficing tradeoffs between resource consumption and output quality.

---

Contract Modes are predefined configurations within the Agent Contracts framework that operationalize satisficing tradeoffs between resource consumption and output quality for common deployment scenarios. The framework defines three standard modes: URGENT, which prioritizes speed and minimizes resource usage at potential cost to output richness; ECONOMICAL, which enforces strict budget limits for cost-sensitive applications; and BALANCED, which seeks a middle ground between quality and efficiency. These modes function as templates that populate the resource constraint and success criteria components of a contract tuple, reducing the engineering burden of configuring individual contracts from scratch. By offering named configurations, Contract Modes make the framework accessible to practitioners who need sensible defaults without deep expertise in formal constraint specification.
