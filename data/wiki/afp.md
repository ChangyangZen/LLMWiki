# Archive of Formal Proofs (AFP)

**Type:** dataset

A community-maintained repository of Isabelle/HOL formal proof developments across mathematics and computer science, used to evaluate the generalization of AutoReal-Prover.

---

The Archive of Formal Proofs (AFP) is a community-maintained online repository of formal proof developments in Isabelle/HOL, covering a broad range of topics in mathematics, computer science, and formal security. Contributions to the AFP undergo editorial review to ensure proof correctness and are archived as citable scientific artifacts. In the AutoReal paper, three AFP projects related to cryptographic security—CRYSTALS-Kyber_Security, RSAPSS, and Elliptic_Curves_Group_Law—are used to evaluate the generalization of AutoReal-Prover beyond the seL4 training domain, collectively comprising 451 theorems. AutoReal-Prover achieves a 53.88% average proof success rate across these AFP projects, demonstrating its ability to transfer to unseen industrial-scale formal verification tasks.
