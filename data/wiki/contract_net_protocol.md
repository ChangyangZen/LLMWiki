# Contract Net Protocol

**Type:** method

A 1980 coordination protocol by Smith that introduced task allocation through bidding contracts in distributed multi-agent systems, serving as the historical foundation for Agent Contracts.

---

The Contract Net Protocol (CNP) is a coordination mechanism introduced by Reid Smith in 1980 that formalized task allocation in distributed multi-agent systems through a bidding and contracting metaphor. In the protocol, a manager node announces available tasks, potential contractor nodes submit bids based on their capabilities, and the manager awards contracts to selected bidders who then execute the tasks. The CNP became a foundational standard in multi-agent systems research and was later formalized by the Foundation for Intelligent Physical Agents (FIPA). Agent Contracts extend this historical metaphor from task allocation into resource-bounded execution, applying the contracting concept to govern how autonomous AI agents consume resources during runtime.
