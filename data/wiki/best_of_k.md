# Best-of-K Evaluation

**Type:** method

A standard evaluation approach for PRMs where the model selects the best solution from K candidate solutions generated by an LLM.

---

Best-of-K is a standard inference-time evaluation protocol for assessing the quality of reward models, including PRMs, in which a generator LLM produces K candidate solutions for a given problem and the reward model selects the one it deems most likely to be correct. This approach serves as a practical measure of a PRM's ability to distinguish correct from incorrect solutions at inference time, without requiring reinforcement learning or other training-time integration. In the FoVer paper, Best-of-K evaluation is used across 12 benchmarks to compare FoVer-PRMs against baseline PRMs and a random selection baseline. Performance improvements under Best-of-K provide evidence that FoVer-PRMs have learned meaningful step-level quality signals transferable across task types.
