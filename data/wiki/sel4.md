# seL4 Verification Project

**Type:** dataset

A high-assurance microkernel verified in Isabelle/HOL via refinement-based proofs, comprising roughly 200,000 lines of Isabelle scripts organized into ten proof categories.

---

The seL4 Verification Project is a high-assurance microkernel formally verified in Isabelle/HOL using a refinement-based methodology, representing one of the most significant achievements in industrial-scale software verification. Its Isabelle proof corpus comprises roughly 200,000 lines of proof scripts organized across ten distinct proof categories, covering functional correctness, security properties, and system invariants. The scale and complexity of seL4's verification artifacts make it a challenging and representative benchmark for evaluating automated theorem proving methods in realistic industrial settings. In the AutoReal paper, 660 seL4 theorems are used as the primary evaluation benchmark, with proof traces from the corpus also serving as training data for the Proof CoT Dataset.
