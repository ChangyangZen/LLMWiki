# AR-LSAT Dataset

**Type:** dataset

A dataset based on analytical reasoning questions from the LSAT exam, requiring complex legal and logical inference.

---

AR-LSAT is a dataset derived from the analytical reasoning section of the Law School Admission Test (LSAT), comprising problems that require complex logical inference over structured scenarios involving ordering, grouping, and selection constraints. The problems are notable for their difficulty and the multi-step conditional reasoning they demand, making them a challenging benchmark for both human test-takers and AI systems. Within the VERGE evaluation, AR-LSAT assesses the framework's ability to handle semi-formal reasoning tasks grounded in legal and procedural logic. Strong performance on AR-LSAT indicates robustness of the verification and refinement pipeline under conditions of high reasoning complexity and ambiguous natural language specification.
