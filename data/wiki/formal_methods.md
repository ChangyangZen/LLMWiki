# Formal Methods

**Type:** concept

Rigorous mathematical techniques for specifying and verifying critical systems, with theorem proving as a key approach requiring substantial expert effort in industrial-scale projects.

---

Formal Methods are mathematically rigorous techniques for the specification, development, and verification of software and hardware systems, providing strong correctness guarantees that complement or replace conventional testing. Theorem proving is a central formal method in which the correctness of a system is established by constructing machine-checked mathematical proofs, as exemplified by the seL4 microkernel verification project. Industrial-scale formal verification projects such as seL4 require substantial expert effort, with proof corpora spanning hundreds of thousands of lines of proof scripts and demanding deep domain knowledge. Automating parts of this process through LLM-driven theorem proving, as pursued in AutoReal, is a key research direction for making formal methods more accessible and cost-effective in industrial practice.
