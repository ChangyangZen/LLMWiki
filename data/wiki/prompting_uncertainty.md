# Prompting Uncertainty

**Type:** concept

Uncertainty arising from variation in how an actor formulates a prompt, including language choice, phrasing, formatting, and typographic differences.

---

Prompting uncertainty refers to the variability in LLM outputs that arises from differences in how an actor formulates the input prompt. Even when the underlying communicative intent is identical, variations in language choice, phrasing, formatting, level of detail, or typographic differences can lead to substantially different generated outputs. This source of uncertainty reflects the sensitivity of LLMs to surface-level prompt characteristics and is formally captured in the framework by modeling the prompt itself as a stochastic process over a set of possible prompt formulations. Prompting uncertainty is particularly relevant in settings where prompts are authored by diverse users or generated automatically, as it contributes to the overall unpredictability of the system's responses.
