# Epistemic Uncertainty

**Type:** concept

Uncertainty due to lack of knowledge, such as gaps in LLM training data, unclear prompt goals, or differing backgrounds among human judges.

---

Epistemic uncertainty is uncertainty that arises from incomplete or insufficient knowledge and is, in principle, reducible by acquiring more information. In LLM text generation, epistemic uncertainty can stem from gaps in the model's training data, ambiguous or underspecified prompts, or differences in the background knowledge and interpretive frameworks of human judges evaluating outputs. Within the formal framework, epistemic uncertainty manifests across all three stages of the pipeline: a user uncertain about how to phrase a prompt, a model uncertain about facts not well-represented in training, or a judge uncertain about the correct evaluation criterion. Unlike aleatoric uncertainty, epistemic uncertainty can potentially be reduced through better training, clearer prompt design, or more consistent evaluation protocols.
