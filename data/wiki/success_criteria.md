# Success Criteria (Φ)

**Type:** concept

Weighted measurable conditions in an Agent Contract that define fulfillment, requiring a weighted sum of satisfied conditions to meet or exceed a threshold θ.

---

Success Criteria (Φ) are weighted, measurable conditions within an Agent Contract that formally define what constitutes successful task fulfillment. Fulfillment is achieved when the weighted sum of satisfied individual conditions meets or exceeds a predefined threshold θ, enabling partial credit and flexible definitions of acceptable outcomes. This design directly operationalizes Herbert Simon's principle of satisficing, allowing agents to achieve sufficiently good results rather than requiring perfect maximization of all objectives. By making success conditions explicit and quantifiable, Φ enables automated contract evaluation and supports the transition of a contract's lifecycle state from ACTIVE to FULFILLED.
