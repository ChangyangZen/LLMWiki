# Natural Questions (NQ)

**Type:** dataset

A single-hop question answering benchmark based on real Google search queries, used to evaluate MiCP's coverage and efficiency.

---

Natural Questions (NQ) is a large-scale question answering benchmark derived from real user queries submitted to the Google search engine, paired with answers extracted from Wikipedia articles. The dataset represents naturalistic, single-hop information needs and is widely used as a standard evaluation benchmark in the open-domain QA community. In the MiCP paper, Natural Questions is used to evaluate the framework's coverage and efficiency on single-hop questions, where the expected benefit of multi-turn reasoning is more modest compared to multi-hop benchmarks. MiCP's strong performance on NQ demonstrates that the framework does not incur unnecessary overhead on questions that can often be resolved in fewer turns.
